Urban Farming - Everyone can get involved

Through community building and redefining social responsibility, I believe we can change the world for the better.

Small Plots

If you have a small living space one of the ways you can get involved is by using using whatever extra space you have to grow greens and veggies.
Since you are not able to contribute large spaces to the community, your social responsibilty lies in reducing, as much as possible, the amount of food you buy.
We are lucky to live in such an advanced technological society as it give us endless access to so many different solutions!

Medium Plots

If you have a living space and a small to medium garden you are one of the luckier people! Somehow the random nature of events has led
to you living where and how you live today. Since you have slightly more space to contribute to the community, your social responsibility
lies in you reducing the amounnt of food you buy AND contributing the space you have to the network of growers in your area.
Isn't that beautiful! any excess food you may produce will be circulated to those who don't have the ability to grow food for themselves.

Large Plots

If you are one of those even luckier people who have found yourself living on a large plot of land then you, my friends,
have the greatest social responsibility of us all. You have to reduce the amount of food you buy to almost nothing.
You then need to contribute all food that you don't require to the network of food growers in your area.
This is where most of the food will be produced for the communities! Isn't that so cool!
Those with the most access to resources help the rest of their communities by making sure everyone has something to eat!
